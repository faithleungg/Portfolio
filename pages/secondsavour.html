Me

Second Savour is a food startup repurposing imperfect produce into healthy candy alternatives through the product line, “Citrus Treats.”

As the sole designer, I created the visual identity and creative direction for the brand. My work includes product packaging, brand assets, and digital marketing.

The Problem

Originating as a university project, Second Savour needed to evolve into a market-ready product.

Minimalism was cool, but didn't reflect the sweet and fun nature of candy (this was the previous packaging).

Research

I conducted a brand forensic, looking for four qualities:
visceral appeal, legibility, ethos, and legacy.

What I Found

Candy brands often rely on strong taglines.
Value propositions should be communicated upfront.
Design with the intention to scale across multiple flavours.

First Iteration

My first iteration was bold, showcasing sticker elements and angled labels to convey playfulness. I chose a bright orange background to represent the upcycled orange peels used.

Second Iteration

Customers didn't understand the angled labels, so I removed them. I introduced a mascot to bring personality to the brand and selected a new typeface that would become the brand’s defining font.

However, the design still felt incohesive, and the brand started to introduce new flavours. The product struggled to sell itself without a pitch.

Solution

I revised the stickers with descriptive product words, paired type hierarchies together, reincorporated the mascot, and reiterated copy writing to highlight the product rather than the company.

Second Savour's new packaging scales across multiple flavours, combining illustrative styles with striking colours to communicate a refreshing, down to earth personality.

To take it one step further, I ran 70+ marketing strategies through social media to grow brand awareness.

This opened the space for more personal experiences with our audience, keeping them updated with the business. It also drove traffic towards e-commerce sales!

What I Learned

Working as the sole designer at a startup required a lot of self-learning and initiative. I learned why justifying desgin decisions mattered, ensuring content was meaningful, especially since creating a sellable product meant every element I added could directly influence the company's sales.
At the same time, being part of a small team allowed me to collaborate cross-functionally and gain insight into areas beyond design, including product strategy, content creation and marketing.
